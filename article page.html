Linus Torvalds

The creator of linux


Biography:

Linus Torvalds (born December 28, 1969, in Helsinki, Finland) is a Finnish-American software engineer and the creator of the Linux kernel, the core of the Linux operating system. He began programming at age 10, fueled by his grandfather's Commodore VIC-20. While studying computer science at the University of Helsinki, he became dissatisfied with the MS-DOS operating system on his PC and decided to create a UNIX-like alternative, which he named Linux. He released the source code freely, fostering a collaborative open-source development model that has made Linux one of the most popular operating systems in the world.

Achievements:
  • 1991 - created the linux and the version control system git
  • 2004 - Named one of the "100 Most influential People in the World" by Time magzine in 2004
  • 2010 - Received an honorary Doctorate from the University of Helsinki in 2010
  • 2013 - Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame in 2013 for his contributions to the development of the Linux kernel and the creation of Git
  • 2014 - Inducted into the Hall of Fame of the Computer History Museum in 2014
  • 2015 - Received the 2015 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2019 - Received the 2019 ACM SIGOPS Hall of Fame Award
  • 2021 - Awarded the Takeda Foundation's 2021 Takeda Award for Social Contribution, which recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to society through technology
  • 2021 - Received the 2021 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award, which recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to the field of consumer electronics
